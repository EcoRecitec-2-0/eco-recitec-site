Frequently Asked Questions

What is Circular Economy?

The Circular Economy: An Ongoing Revolution

The circular economy is not just a trend; it is a revolution. Imagine a system where nothing is wasted, where resources are reused, regenerated, and reinvented. It is a perfect dance between nature and technology, where materials flow in closed loops, avoiding depletion and environmental degradation.

Skills of the Future: Beyond Disciplinary Boundaries

In this project, we are not just engineers, entrepreneurs, psychologists, designers, or economists. We are bridge builders, connecting diverse skills to create a more resilient tomorrow. Sustainability professionals, technologists, psychologists, and human sciences experts will be the architects of this transformation.

Technology as an Ally

Here, technology is not just a tool; it is our ally in the pursuit of intelligent solutions. Simple software, such as that from the Flashplayhr platform, can promote a mindset shift that facilitates the transition from a linear economy to a circular economy. Blockchain technology tracks the origin of materials, while artificial intelligence optimizes and better integrates segments, people, content, and processes. Augmented reality allows us to visualize a circular world even before we build it.

Circular Tech Skills

Psychology and Humanity at the Heart of Change

We cannot forget the human factor. Psychology teaches us to overcome resistance, inspire behavior change, and build a circular mindset. After all, the circular economy is not just about materials; it's about people, communities, and well-being.

Motivational Factors: Smart and Durable Design, Reimagining Products

Here, creativity is our ally. How can we design products that last longer, are easy to repair, and can be disassembled at the end of their useful life? Let's explore innovative materials, smart geometries, and designs that transcend planned obsolescence.

Mindset and Circular Organizational Design

Here we will introduce technologies to help rethink processes that need to move from linearity to a systemic and circular vision.
